Embracing Calm: Understanding and Navigating Anxiety

Dec 13 2024 Health and Therapy 3 Comments

Dr james Gitau

Anxiety is a shared human experience, a sensation most individuals encounter at various stages of their lives. It often appears as a not-so-easy anticipation of upcoming events, an awful, overwhelming feeling of uncertainty and worry about the future, the what-could-be disasters that are almost too awful to imagine. In the wake of a stressful event, we might feel anxious, and for good reasonâ€”our bodies are designed to respond to stress with an alertness that helps us cope with immediate dangers. But some of us live with a baseline level of anxiety that is out of proportion to anything happening in our current lives and that is also, unfortunately, rather life-disrupting.

The reasons for anxiety are as diverse as the individuals who feel it. It can have roots in past trauma, a genetic predisposition, or a combination of environmental stressors. Sometimes, it strikes without a clear or discernable cause, an unwelcome companion that pushes its way in and sets up camp. A person who feels this kind of anxiety might often question their feelings and wonder why it's so tough to "calm down" or "get over it." This self-blame adds another burden to our already heavy emotional backpack. Anxiety works its way into every nook and cranny of our lives. It doesn't care about our age, gender, or background. It can travel with us to any part of our day and get comfortable as we try to move from room to room. Its intensity can range from a mild tickle to a full-blown panic attack that leaves us gasping for air. It rewards no one with an escape.

Even though anxiety can feel overwhelming, it is not impossible to overcome. Taking small steps, such as practicing mindfulness, can help. Seeking support from friends, family, or professionals can also be a big help. For some, anxiety may warrant the use of antidepressants or other forms of medication. Meanwhile, cognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT) has been shown in several studies to be a highly effective treatment for anxiety. In moments of intense anxiety, it can be helpful to remind yourself that this feeling, as overwhelming as it seems, is temporary. Emotions ebb and flow like waves, and even the most turbulent storm eventually subsides. You are not alone in this experience, and reaching out for help is a courageous and worthwhile act. Every moment spent in self-compassion and self-awareness is a step toward reclaiming your peace of mind. By seeking understanding, both within yourself and through connection with others, you can begin to navigate anxiety with resilience and hope, transforming it from a source of fear into a catalyst for personal growth.
